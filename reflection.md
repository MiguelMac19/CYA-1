# Reflection 

## Activity Description

For this assignment I applied to four full time positions through Indeed that aligned with my goal of getting into IT and tech support after graduating. The expectation was to find and apply to career-relevant jobs and reflect on the process. I applied to POS Support Technician I at United Merchant Services in Lynnwood, Fiber Technician I at Forged Fiber 37 in Seattle, Arcade Game Technician at MRJN Associates in Sunnyside, and IT Help Desk Technician 1 at Universal Language Service in Bellevue. Each application required me to read through the job description, decide if my background was a match, and submit my resume through Indeed.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

The biggest decision I had to make was which roles to actually apply to. I searched for IT support and technician positions in Washington state and tried to pick roles where my skills made sense. I have my Google IT Support Professional Certificate and I am working toward my CompTIA Security+, so I looked for jobs that mentioned hardware, troubleshooting, or technical support.

The IT Help Desk Technician role at Universal Language Service was the most obvious fit since it directly matches what I want to do short term. The POS Support Technician role at United Merchant Services was interesting because point of sale systems are everywhere in business and that kind of hands-on hardware and software troubleshooting is core IT work. The Fiber Technician role at Forged Fiber 37 was a stretch but networking and infrastructure knowledge is something I want to build since it connects to both sysadmin and cybersecurity work long term. The Arcade Game Technician at MRJN Associates was a bit different but it still involves diagnosing and fixing technical hardware problems which is a real skill in IT.

I also had to decide how to present myself. Since I am a graduating senior with more coursework than work experience, I made sure my resume highlighted my certifications and the technical labs I have done in school rather than just listing jobs.

## Contributions

This was an independent activity. I searched for roles, evaluated each one on my own, and submitted all four applications myself.

## Quality Assessment

I think the applications I submitted were reasonable for where I am right now as a new grad. If I were to do this again I would apply to many more positions since a bigger volume increases the chances of getting a response and I would search for more entry level roles. I would also spend more time customizing my cover letter for each role instead of using a general one. 

## Career Alignment

This experience moved me closer to my career goals because it forced me to actually look at what is out there and what employers want. I noticed that most IT support roles ask for experience with ticketing systems, Active Directory, and basic networking. Those are areas I have touched in school but need more hands on practice with. Knowing that now helps me focus my studying before I graduate.

My short term goal is to get into IT support and my long term goal is to move into cybersecurity. Several of the roles I applied to like the help desk and POS support positions are exactly the kind of entry level tech work that builds the foundation for security work later.
